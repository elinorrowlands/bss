Biodivergent Sites & Sounds is an autoethnographic investigation of place, of waterways and of autism as a cultural identity.

An autistic-led immersive and accessible experience for audiences to experience nature and to engage in the preservation of Harlesden Canal.

The project was led by multi-disciplinary artist Elinor Rowlands in collaboration with the Canal & River Trust (and its many volunteers); fellow neurodivergent artists Jo-anne Cox, Dee Fry, R.Dyer and Ben Hajioff; young people from Harlesden Primary School and Sport at the Heart through the Young Brent Foundation; and creative technologist Charles Matthews.

It was kindly funded by Arts Council England and the Canal & River Trust, with support from Cole’s Gallery and Creative Health Camden.
